🔗 Cybersecurity Processes Are the Glue That Binds

Maintaining effective cybersecurity processes is too critical to an organization to leave to chance, yet many organizations continue to rely on undocumented processes, tribal knowledge, and paying security professionals to manage routine operational security controls.

⚠️ Critical Truth: Cybersecurity processes form the critical piece between those performing the security function and the technology.

The Process Trinity

🎯 Objectives

Processes are developed to meet specific objectives through creation of multiple outputs

📋 Practices

Include documented practices and activities that ensure consistency

📊 Outputs

Generate consistent, quality outputs regardless of who performs them

👥 Undocumented Processes = Tribal Knowledge Dependency

Some organizations operate with processes that are either ill-defined or undocumented, resulting in inconsistent activities and different outputs of differing quality, depending on the individual performing the process.

The Vulnerability Management Example

Scenario: Two Employees, Two Approaches

Employee A:
  • Uses Tool A
  • Scans monthly
  • Patches critical vulnerabilities in 7 days
Employee B:
  • Uses Tool B
  • Different schedule
  • Fixes all vulnerabilities in 60 days

Result: Different outcomes, increased risk, inability to articulate organization's risk posture

Problems with Undocumented Processes

  1. Assumption trap: Everyone assumes others are doing the same thing
  2. No improvement: Processes cannot be universally improved
  3. Time waste: Constant re-communication of processes
  4. Knowledge gap: Junior members can't learn from senior best practices
  5. Reinvention: The wheel is reinvented again and again
  6. Accountability void: Uncertainty about who owns and is accountable

Why Particular Attention to Cybersecurity Processes?

💡 Simple Answer: Even the slightest failure in one of these processes can cause issues with confidential disclosure, availability, or data integrity of the systems in place to support the mission.

No Intrinsic Motivation to Document

Information technology professionals generally dislike creating documentation because it takes time away from:

  • 🚀 Exploring new technology
  • 💻 Creating new applications and databases
  • 🔧 Resolving system or end-user issues

Without clear direction and governance to ensure process development is an organizational priority, these processes are unlikely to be created.

Move Routine Actions to Operations

Information security personnel are more expensive resources relative to computer operations areas. These resources should be leveraged to:

  • Design the most appropriate processes
  • Move processes to production operation ASAP
  • Execute via less expensive resources (SOC, MSSP)
  • Focus on exception reports and further analysis

This frees cybersecurity professionals to focus on high-value efforts versus routine operational work.

Cybersecurity Practices and Activities

Example: Manage Endpoint Security Practice

Purpose: Ensure endpoints (laptop, desktop, server, mobile devices) are secured at a level equal to or greater than defined security requirements.

Inputs:

  • Information security architecture
  • Service-level agreements
  • Physical inventory audits
  • Reports of security violations

Activities:

  • Configure endpoints securely
  • Categorize endpoint types and control needs
  • Identify potential entry points
  • Analyze target attractiveness
  • Implement network monitoring
  • Dispose of endpoints securely
  • Examine attack history

🎯 The Five COBIT 5 Domains

Different types of cybersecurity processes work together across five domains:

EDM

Evaluate, Direct, and Monitor

Provides governance for cybersecurity, ensuring appropriate direction and monitoring mechanisms.

  • Governance framework maintenance
  • Benefits delivery
  • Risk optimization
  • Resource optimization
  • Stakeholder transparency

Example: Addressing obsolete IT technology through EDM04 - Resource Management Direction and/or Oversight

APO

Align, Plan, and Organize

Contains cybersecurity management processes to embed cybersecurity within IT management framework.

  • Align cybersecurity strategy
  • Define architectural components
  • Manage cybersecurity portfolio
  • Set budget and provision for breaches
  • Manage training process
  • Obtain vendor SLAs
  • Identify risks and treatment plans
  • Manage innovation with new technologies
BAI

Build, Acquire, and Implement

Defines process capabilities to assist in executing the cybersecurity program.

  • Define cybersecurity requirements
  • Select cybersecurity solutions
  • Embed cybersecurity in change management
  • Manage normal and emergency changes
  • Manage collective knowledge
  • Manage requirements risk

Project management practices ensure solutions meet business requirements timely and within budget.

DSS

Deliver, Service, and Support

Provides operational support and "keeps the cybersecurity lights on."

  • Operations management
  • Incident identification and management
  • Ticketing system management
  • Root cause analysis
  • Crisis management
  • Business continuity planning
  • Disaster recovery

Key Control: Restoration of data from backups is critical for ransomware recovery.

MEA

Monitor, Evaluate, and Assess

Provides cybersecurity monitoring, self-assessments, and compliance reporting.

  • Cybersecurity monitoring
  • Self-assessments
  • Compliance reporting
  • Periodic reviews
  • Corrective action tracking
  • Performance reporting
  • Internal control mechanisms

🔄 COBIT 5 Domains Support Complete Cybersecurity Lifecycle

Each COBIT 5 domain contributes to maturing the cybersecurity program processes by contributing either governance or management practices to address planning, building, or ongoing operation.

💡 Key Value: Processes are enablers that provide the who, what, when, and where actions needed. Holistically, this reduces the risk that actions necessary to protect confidentiality, integrity, and availability are missed.

Why Use COBIT 5 Process Enabler Approach?

Comparison with Other Approaches

NIST SP 800-53:
  • Very prescriptive guidelines for federal systems
  • Can be overwhelming for many organizations
  • Best used to complement and develop organization-specific activities
CIS Top 20 Critical Controls:
  • Technical-based controls (inventories, configurations, vulnerabilities)
  • Prioritized by control importance
  • Less focus on business objectives and process development
ISO/IEC 27001 & ISF Standards:
  • Can supplement COBIT 5 processes
  • Mapped to COBIT 5 Framework in appendices
  • Provide additional depth in specific areas
⚡ COBIT 5 Advantage: Provides overarching governance and management assurance that adequate cybersecurity coverage exists from governance and planning through ongoing operation and measurement.

✅ What Does CEO Tom Get Out of Process Enablers?

Using COBIT 5 process enablers provides a holistic set of cybersecurity processes to manage the cyber risk management system. Once Tom has implemented these processes:

📋 Clear Accountability

It will be clear who is accountable and responsible for each governance and management practice, and who needs to be informed or consulted (RACIResponsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed - a matrix for defining roles charts).

🎯 Defined Practices

Clear definition of cybersecurity governance and management practices necessary to achieve each process that makes up the cybersecurity program.

✅ Good Practice Activities

Assurance that detailed activities are defined and based on good practices, leveraging technical definitions from other standards.

🛡️ Risk Mitigation

Comfort that processes are in place to ensure risks inherent in implementing technology have associated processes to mitigate them.

💎 Value Creation

Resources spent on executing processes add value by creating cybersecurity-specific outputs used as inputs to follow-on processes.

📊 Integrated Program

An integrated program covering multiple processes supporting the organization, people, and technology with metrics to measure effectiveness and efficiency.

🚀 The Ultimate Outcome

Continual review of processes aids in making clear decisions on cybersecurity priorities and those processes that need additional investment, or those that can be discontinued or moved to lower cost support.

Tom will have an integrated program that holistically supports business objectives with clear metrics for measuring program effectiveness and efficiency.

🎓 Conclusion

The following cyber risk management statement represents those organization capabilities CEO and board expect to be demonstrated in terms of treating cyber risk using process capabilities:

📋 TREATING CYBER RISKS USING PROCESS CAPABILITIES

Cybersecurity process capabilities provide the governance and management practices necessary to effectively and efficiently align the cybersecurity program with the business enterprise objectives.

Detailed activities are developed to support the cybersecurity practices to provide:

  • Governance (evaluate, direct, and monitor)
  • Manage (align, plan, and organize the work)
  • Create solutions (build, acquire, and implement)
  • Sustain (deliver, service, and support)
  • Improve (monitor, evaluate, and assess)

These processes form a cybersecurity lifecycle with defined inputs and outputs based on generally accepted good practices that, taken together holistically, can serve to reduce the organizational cybersecurity risk.

🎯 The Bottom Line

Processes are the glue that binds cybersecurity together - without documented processes, organizations rely on tribal knowledge and face inconsistent protection.
