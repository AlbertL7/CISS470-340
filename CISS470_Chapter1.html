üé≠ Understanding Threat Actors: The "Baddies" in Detail ‚ñ∂

Cybercriminals - The Most Prevalent Threat

  • Motivation: Financial gain through ransomware, data theft, fraud
  • Methods: Social engineering as primary attack vector, phishing, malware
  • Evolution: Now operating as sophisticated business enterprises with:
    • Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) models
    • Cryptocurrency laundering operations
    • Professional negotiators for ransom payments
    • Customer service desks for victims

State-Level Actors - Advanced Persistent Threats

  • Motivation: Espionage, intellectual property theft, critical infrastructure disruption
  • Capabilities: Significant resources, custom tools, long-term persistence
  • Targets: Government agencies, defense contractors, critical infrastructure, technology companies
  • Characteristics: Patient, stealthy, willing to maintain presence for months or years

Hacktivists - Ideologically Motivated

  • Motivation: Political or social causes, public embarrassment of targets
  • Methods: Website defacement, data leaks, DDoS attacks
  • Impact: Reputation damage, operational disruption, public relations crises

Insider Threats - The Enemy Within

  • Types: Malicious insiders, negligent employees, compromised accounts
  • Access: Already inside the security perimeter with legitimate credentials
  • Detection Challenge: Activities often appear as normal business operations
  • Statistics: Responsible for approximately 30% of data breaches

üè¢ Key Stakeholder Groups and Their Roles

üé© Board & Executive Level Responsibilities ‚ñ∂

Board of Directors

  • Primary Role: Oversight and strategic direction
  • Accountable for: Overall governance and fiduciary duty
  • Key Activities: Approving risk appetite, reviewing performance, ensuring compliance
  • RASCI Position: Usually "A" for governance, "I" for operations

Chief Executive Officer (CEO)

  • Primary Role: Ultimate accountability for cyber risk
  • Accountable for: Enterprise cyber resilience and incident response
  • Key Message: "The buck stops here"
  • RASCI Position: "A" for most strategic cyber decisions

Audit Committee

  • Primary Role: Independent assurance and compliance verification
  • Consulted on: Control effectiveness, audit findings
  • Reports to: Board of Directors
  • RASCI Position: "C" for controls, "I" for incidents
‚öñÔ∏è Risk & Compliance Functions ‚ñ∂

Risk Management

  • Responsible for: Integrating cyber risk into Enterprise Risk Management
  • Key Activities: Risk assessments, appetite setting, reporting
  • Collaboration: Works with all business units for risk identification
  • RASCI Position: "R" for risk processes, "C" for treatments

Legal Department

  • Consulted on: Regulatory requirements, breach notifications
  • Accountable for: Legal compliance and contract management
  • Critical During: Incident response and breach management
  • RASCI Position: "A" for compliance, "C" for incidents

Internal Audit

  • Role: Independent assurance provider (Third Line of Defense)
  • Never: Responsible for operational activities
  • Reports to: Audit Committee independently
  • RASCI Position: Never "R" for operations, always independent
üíª Operations & Technology Teams ‚ñ∂

Information Technology (IT)

  • Responsible for: Technical implementation of controls
  • Key Activities: System administration, patching, monitoring
  • Challenge: Often seen as sole owner (incorrectly)
  • RASCI Position: "R" for technical controls, "S" for business initiatives

Information Security

  • Responsible for: Security operations and monitoring
  • Led by: Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
  • Activities: Threat intelligence, incident response, security architecture
  • RASCI Position: "R" for security operations, "C" for business decisions

Business Operations

  • Responsible for: Business continuity and recovery
  • Owns: Business processes and operational risk
  • Critical for: Defining recovery objectives (RTO/RPO)
  • RASCI Position: "A" for business process security
ü§ù Support Functions Critical Role ‚ñ∂

Human Resources

  • Drives: Culture change and security awareness training
  • Responsible for: Background checks, onboarding, termination procedures
  • Partners with IT on: Insider threat programs
  • RASCI Position: "R" for people security, "A" for culture

Finance

  • Manages: Cybersecurity budgets and investments
  • Responsible for: Cyber insurance procurement
  • Key Role: Risk quantification and cost-benefit analysis
  • RASCI Position: "R" for budget, "C" for risk transfer

Corporate Communications

  • Handles: Crisis communication and stakeholder messaging
  • Critical during: Breach notifications and media response
  • Prepares: Communication plans and templates
  • RASCI Position: "R" for external communications

ü§ù Cross-Functional Collaboration Examples

  • Breach Response: Legal coordinates with ALL functions for notification and remediation
