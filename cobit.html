COBIT Learning Center

What is COBIT?

COBIT (Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies) is a framework created by ISACA for the governance and management of enterprise IT. It helps organizations achieve their objectives through effective governance and management of IT.

Key Features

  • Comprehensive framework covering the enterprise end-to-end
  • Provides a holistic approach to governance and management
  • Enables IT to be governed and managed in an integrated way
  • Helps optimize IT investments and manage IT-related risk

COBIT 5 Principles

COBIT 5 is based on five key principles:

1. Meeting Stakeholder Needs

Enterprises exist to create value for stakeholders by maintaining a balance between benefits, risk, and resource use.

2. Covering the Enterprise End-to-End

COBIT integrates governance of enterprise IT into enterprise governance and covers all functions and processes.

3. Applying a Single Integrated Framework

COBIT aligns with other relevant standards and frameworks at a high level, allowing the enterprise to use it as an overarching framework.

4. Enabling a Holistic Approach

Efficient governance and management require a holistic approach using the seven enablers.

5. Separating Governance From Management

COBIT makes a clear distinction between governance and management, which require different structures and serve different purposes.

COBIT Process Reference Model

COBIT 5 includes 37 governance and management processes organized into 5 domains:

Governance Domain

  • EDM (Evaluate, Direct and Monitor): 5 processes for governance activities

Management Domains

  • APO (Align, Plan and Organize): 13 processes for IT strategy and planning
  • BAI (Build, Acquire and Implement): 10 processes for solution delivery
  • DSS (Deliver, Service and Support): 6 processes for service delivery and support
  • MEA (Monitor, Evaluate and Assess): 3 processes for performance monitoring

COBIT Implementation Lifecycle

COBIT provides a structured approach to implementation through seven phases:

Phase 1: Initiate Programme

  • Identify drivers and obtain management buy-in
  • Establish governance structures

Phase 2: Define Problems and Opportunities

  • Assess current state
  • Define pain points

Phase 3: Define Road Map

  • Set improvement targets
  • Analyze gaps

Phase 4: Plan Programme

  • Define projects
  • Develop improvement plan

Phase 5-6: Execute and Monitor

  • Implement improvements
  • Monitor progress and performance

Phase 7: Sustain

  • Embed new approaches
  • Monitor and improve continuously

Benefits of Implementing COBIT

For the Enterprise

  • Maintain high-quality information for decision-making
  • Achieve strategic goals through effective IT use
  • Achieve operational excellence through reliable technology
  • Maintain IT-related risk at acceptable levels
  • Optimize cost of IT services and technology

For IT Management

  • Clear ownership and accountability through RACI charts
  • Clear view on how to organize IT functions
  • Better planning and monitoring tools
  • Improved communication with business stakeholders

For Auditors

  • Comprehensive control objectives
  • Clear audit trails and accountability
  • Standardized assessment approach
  • Risk-based audit planning
