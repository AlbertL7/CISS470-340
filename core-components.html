üè¢ Organisation: Strategic Leadership & Direction

Overview

The Organisation pillar represents the strategic apex of governance, encompassing board oversight, executive leadership, and the structural frameworks that guide enterprise direction.

Core Responsibilities

  • Vision & Strategy: Setting long-term organizational direction and strategic objectives that align with stakeholder expectations
  • Governance Structure: Establishing board committees, reporting lines, and accountability frameworks
  • Risk Appetite: Defining acceptable risk levels and tolerance thresholds across the enterprise
  • Resource Allocation: Strategic distribution of capital, human resources, and technology investments
  • Performance Oversight: Monitoring organizational performance against strategic objectives and risk tolerances

Risk Management Role

  • Ownership of the enterprise risk management framework
  • Setting tone at the top for risk culture
  • Ensuring adequate resources for risk management
  • Regular review of risk reports and escalations
  • Crisis management and business continuity oversight

Governance Mechanisms

  • Board risk committee charter and operations
  • Executive risk committees and forums
  • Strategic planning processes
  • Policy development and approval
  • Stakeholder communication strategies

üë• People: Human Capital & Culture

Overview

The People pillar encompasses all human elements within the organization, from individual competencies to collective culture, driving execution and innovation while managing behavioral risks.

Key Components

  • Workforce Composition: Employees, contractors, management, and board members with diverse skills and perspectives
  • Competency Management: Skills assessment, capability development, and succession planning
  • Cultural Development: Values, beliefs, and behaviors that characterize the organization
  • Performance Management: Goal setting, evaluation, feedback, and recognition systems
  • Change Leadership: Guiding organizational transformation and adaptation

Risk Culture Elements

  • Risk awareness and education programs
  • Speak-up culture and psychological safety
  • Ethical decision-making frameworks
  • Learning from failures and near-misses
  • Risk-adjusted performance metrics

People Risk Management

  • Insider threat prevention and detection
  • Behavioral risk assessment and monitoring
  • Key person risk and succession planning
  • Training effectiveness measurement
  • Culture assessment and transformation

‚öôÔ∏è Process: Operations & Controls

Overview

The Process pillar provides the operational framework through which strategy is executed, ensuring consistency, efficiency, and control across all organizational activities.

Process Architecture

  • Core Processes: Value-creating activities that directly serve customers and stakeholders
  • Support Processes: Internal processes that enable core operations
  • Management Processes: Planning, monitoring, and control activities
  • Improvement Processes: Continuous improvement and innovation mechanisms

Control Framework

  • Preventive controls to avoid risks before they occur
  • Detective controls to identify issues quickly
  • Corrective controls to address problems effectively
  • Compensating controls for residual risks
  • Monitoring and testing of control effectiveness

Process Governance

  • Process ownership and accountability models
  • Standard operating procedures and work instructions
  • Process performance metrics and KPIs
  • Change management procedures
  • Process audit and assurance programs

üíª Technology: Digital Infrastructure & Innovation

Overview

The Technology pillar encompasses all digital assets, systems, and capabilities that enable modern business operations while introducing unique risks and opportunities.

Technology Components

  • Core Systems: ERP, CRM, and operational platforms that run the business
  • Data Infrastructure: Storage, processing, analytics, and reporting capabilities
  • Network & Security: Connectivity, access controls, and protective technologies
  • Emerging Technologies: AI, IoT, blockchain, and other innovative solutions
  • Digital Channels: Customer-facing platforms and interaction points

Technology Risk Areas

  • Cybersecurity threats and vulnerabilities
  • System availability and performance
  • Data privacy and protection
  • Technology obsolescence and technical debt
  • Vendor and third-party dependencies

IT Governance

  • Enterprise architecture standards and guidelines
  • Technology investment decision frameworks
  • Change and release management processes
  • Service level agreements and support models
  • Innovation and digital transformation governance
